- In Nigeria, the exclusive jurisdiction over employment and labour disputes is not vested in a single court but rather distributed across various courts depending on the nature and context of the dispute. Here's a general overview:

1. **High Court**: The High Court of Nigeria generally has jurisdiction over civil matters, including labour disputes. Labour matters can be heard in the High Court if they involve substantial amounts of money or significant legal issues.

2. **Labour Court**: Labour Courts, established under the Labour Act (Cap L 84 Laws of Nigeria, 2004), specifically deal with labour disputes. They have jurisdiction over matters relating to employment contracts, industrial relations, trade unions, and other labour-related issues. These courts are established in each state and the Federal Capital Territory.

3. **Arbitration Tribunals**: The Arbitration and Conciliation Act (Cap A25 Laws of the Federation, 2003) provides for arbitration tribunals which can also handle labour disputes, especially those arising out of employment contracts.

4. **Federal Industrial Court**: The Federal Industrial Court, established under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, has jurisdiction over industrial disputes, including those related to labour and employment, particularly when they involve federal laws or affect interstate commerce.

5. **State Industrial Arbitration Tribunals**: Each state may have its own industrial arbitration tribunal that handles labour disputes within that state.

It's important to note that the jurisdiction can vary based on the specifics of the case, such as the amount of money involved, the parties' locations, and whether federal or state laws are implicated.
