|-\| According to the Labour Act, Cap L1 LFN 2004, section 16, a worker is entitled to be paid wages for up to twelve working days in any calendar year during absence due to temporary illness certified by a registered medical practitioner, provided that the contract remains in existence during the period of absence, the worker is ready and willing to perform their part of the contract except for the incapacity caused by the illness, and the worker consents to be examined by a medical practitioner nominated by the employer. If your two-week illness meets these conditions, and you have provided a doctor's note, your employer is required to pay you for those days. However, since the entitlement is limited to twelve days, if your absence exceeds this period, the payment obligation may not apply. [Labour Act, Cap L1 LFN 2004, s.16].
