Labour Act, Cap L1 LFN 2004, section 15: Wages shall become due and payable at the end of each period for which the contract is expressed to subsist, that is to say, daily, weekly or at such other period as may be agreed upon: Provided that, where the period is more than one month, the wages shall become due and payable at intervals not exceeding one month.

QUESTION: Dem never pay me salary for three months now. Which law dey protect me?

|-\| Na so, my friend. According to Labour Act, Cap L1 LFN 2004, s. 15, wages shall become due and payable at the end of each period for which the contract is expressed to subsist. If dem no pay you for three months, dem no follow di rule. 

Di rule say if period be more than one month, wages shall become due and payable at intervals not exceeding one month. Dat mean if dem no pay you for three months, dem no follow di rule of paying every month. Dat mean dem no follow di law.
